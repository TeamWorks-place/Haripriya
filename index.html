My Design Process

Define

Everything starts with understanding who we're designing for and what we're solving. I deep dive into user goals, business objectives, and pain points. Through stakeholder interviews and user personas, the problem becomes clear. This clarity sets the foundation for everything that follows.

Research

I dig into data and user behaviour to uncover actionable insights. Competitor analysis and usability audits shape the path forward. Quantitative research tells the what, while qualitative reveals the why. This step ensures every decision is backed by empathy and evidence.

Ideate

Here, the magic of brainstorming meets design thinking. I explore a spectrum of solutions, from low-fi sketches to wireframes. Prioritizing user journeys, I map interactions that solve real-world needs. Collaboration ensures ideas are tested before being set in stone.

Design

This is where creativity meets functionality. I craft intuitive, accessible, and visually delightful interfaces. High-fidelity prototypes bring designs to life, considering edge cases too. Through style guides and design systems, consistency takes center stage.

Test & Iterate

User feedback is my compass—testing validates assumptions. Through usability tests and A/B experiments, I uncover improvement areas. Iteration is not an afterthought; it's the engine of refinement. The result? Solutions that delight users and meet business goals.

Detailed Case Studies

St. Joseph’s Hospital

Healthcare Website

Role: UI/UX Designer
Tools: Figma, User Research

Problem Statement

Patients and visitors often need to find relevant hospital services (specialties, diagnostics, contact info) quickly. In a healthcare context — especially when under stress — users should be able to reach what they need without confusion or delay. Healthcare-site UX research indicates that many hospital websites fail due to “overloaded pages with too much text,” “complicated forms / booking processes,” or “unclear navigation.” Potential patients require trust and clarity: they need to feel confident about the hospital’s credibility, its doctors, its facilities, and service offerings. Poor design, outdated visuals or ambiguous information can undermine trust. Many users may access the site from mobile devices; some may have accessibility needs (visual impairment, older age). Failing to ensure mobile-responsiveness, readability, accessibility may discourage or exclude such users. Users want simple, direct paths to key tasks — e.g. booking an appointment, finding contact/locations, viewing relevant services — without having to navigate through complex or cluttered menus.

Solution - UX / UI Strategy & Implementation

To address the user needs and challenges inherent in a hospital website, the design adopts a clean, intuitive information architecture that organizes content into clearly labeled sections (e.g. “Specialties & Doctors”, “Diagnostics & Lab”, “Book Appointment”, “Contact Us”, “Gallery”). The navigation and layout are structured to minimize cognitive load — enabling visitors (patients or caregivers) to locate services, doctor information, facility details or contact options in a few clicks rather than sifting through dense text. Visual communication is kept consistent and professional: a calming colour palette, readable typography, and ample white-space contribute to a sense of trust and clarity — crucial for a healthcare context. Wherever possible, real-world cues (doctor & service lists; facility photos; clear CTAs) are used to build credibility and reassure users that they are engaging with a legitimate medical institution. Key user actions (such as booking an appointment or contacting the hospital) are surfaced via prominent, well-placed calls-to-action (CTAs), reducing friction in critical user journeys. The content is written in plain language, avoiding excessive medical jargon, and organized in digestible sections or bullet-style groupings to enable quick scanning — which is vital given that patients often browse under stress or urgency. Moreover, the design is responsive and mobile-friendly, so that users on smartphones and tablets (common among many patients) receive the same usability and clarity as desktop users. Finally, the interface supports reliability and future-readiness: consistent UI styling across the website, a scalable structure for future expansion (new departments, updated doctor lists, additional patient resources), and design decisions that lay the groundwork for accessibility (e.g. clear contrast, proper content organization) — all to make the site inclusive, user-centric, and maintainable over time.

Outcome

Improved User Experience & Usability: Visitors can easily find what they need — services, doctor info, contact, booking — without confusion or overload. Enhanced Trust and Professional Impression: Clean design and organized content help patients feel confident about hospital professionalism and credibility. Accessibility and Wider Reach: Mobile-friendly, simple UI allows a diverse audience (including older patients or those with limited technical skills) to access and use the site comfortably. Better Conversion Potential: Clear, visible CTAs and straightforward booking/contact flow likely increase number of appointment inquiries, patient leads, or overall patient engagement. Scalable & Maintainable Structure: A well-defined information architecture and consistent UI design make future expansions (e.g. more departments, new services, patient portal, multilingual support) easier to integrate without compromising usability.

lushlayerss

pillows Website

Role: UI/UX Designer
Tools: Figma, User Research

Problem Statement

Need for Clear Product Discovery and Browsing Experience — With a range of apparel categories (casual wear, resort wear, best sellers, new arrivals, etc.), users require an intuitive structure to navigate products easily. Without proper organization and clear visuals, product overload or confusion may deter potential buyers. Need to Instill Trust and Brand Credibility — Since customers cannot physically inspect clothing online, they need reassurance about quality, authenticity, and reliability. Lack of clarity about brand values, product quality, shipping/delivery details, or unclear interface may cause hesitation before purchase. Need for Smooth, Intuitive Checkout & Purchase Flow — For an e-commerce site, friction in adding items to cart, viewing product details, or checking out can lead to abandoned carts. Especially with fashion retail — users expect seamless browsing → selection → checkout. Need for Visual Appeal and Usability Across Devices — Fashion retail depends heavily on visual presentation (images, layout, aesthetics). Users browsing via mobile or desktop expect clarity, good readability, appealing visuals, and fast performance. Poor layout, low-quality images, or slow loading can diminish user experience and conversion.

Solution - UX / UI Strategy & Implementation

To address these needs and challenges, the following design and UX solutions were implemented: Structured Information Architecture & Clear Navigation The website is organized with clear categories (e.g. best sellers, new arrivals, clothing categories), making browsing straightforward. This reduces cognitive load and helps users find relevant products quickly. High-quality Visual Presentation & Product Imagery Products are presented with clean, attractive layouts and high-resolution images to give users a clear view of apparel — essential in fashion e-commerce. This supports visual appeal and helps simulate in-store browsing. Prominent Brand Story & Trust Signals The “About Us” or brand-story sections communicate brand values, commitment to quality and comfort, making the brand appear more credible. Clear shipping, delivery, and payment information (where applicable) build transparency and reduce purchase anxiety. User-oriented Checkout & Conversion Flow The design prioritizes simplicity in the user journey: from product discovery to cart and checkout. Important calls-to-action (e.g. “Shop Now”, “Add to Cart”, “Checkout”) are placed intuitively for ease of use. This reduces friction and supports conversion. This aligns with recommended e-commerce UX practices. Responsive & Accessible Design The layout and UI are designed to function properly on various devices (mobile, tablet, desktop), ensuring consistent usability across platforms — critical given varied user contexts. Balanced Aesthetics and Functionality While the site focuses on clean, minimal, and polished visuals to reflect fashion-brand identity, UX principles guide the functionality: readability, intuitive navigation, clear product information, ensuring that design does not sacrifice usability for style.

Outcome

Improved User Experience & Engagement: Users browsing the site can quickly find products via organized categories; appealing visuals and clear navigation encourage exploration, increasing user engagement. Enhanced Trust & Brand Credibility: Clean design, brand story, transparent information (about shipping/delivery/payment) helps build trust — making users more comfortable ordering online. Higher Conversion Potential: A smooth path from product discovery → selection → checkout (with intuitive CTAs and minimal friction) improves chances of purchase, reducing cart abandonment risk. Scalable & Maintainable Platform: The structured architecture and consistent UI make it easier to add new collections, update products, manage content, and maintain usability as the store grows. Accessibility & Cross-device Compatibility: Responsive design ensures customers on mobile or desktop get similarly good experience — broadening user reach and supporting inclusivity.

Tattvo Tech

Corporate Website

Role: UI/UX Designer
Tools: Figma

Problem Statement

Need for Clear Service Discovery and Easy Navigation — The company offers multiple complex services (web development, backend, CRM, cloud). Without a clear information architecture and intuitive navigation, potential clients may get overwhelmed or unable to find what they need. Need to Establish Professional Credibility & Trust — For an IT services firm, clients base their decision on perceived professionalism, reliability, and clarity about services. A poorly designed or generic website may deter trust — reducing lead generation. Need for a Seamless Conversion Path (Lead/Contact Flow) — Users (business decision-makers) visiting the site must be able to quickly understand offerings and contact the company (via “Get in Touch” or contact form) with minimal friction. Need for Clarity, Readability, and Appeal Across Devices — Prospective clients may access the site from desktop or mobile. Website must communicate complex service offerings in a clean, readable, and visually appealing format across devices.

Solution - UX / UI Strategy & Implementation

To address these needs, the following design and UX decisions were made: Structured Information Architecture & Clear Navigation The website is organized with clear sections: Home (overview), Services (listing Web Development, Backend, CRM, Cloud, etc.), About Us (company mission, team), and Contact / Get in Touch. This ensures that visitors can quickly locate relevant services without navigating through clutter. Professional, Clean Visual Design Reflecting Brand Identity The UI uses a modern and minimal design language — a restrained color palette, clean typography, and consistent spacing/layout — to reflect professionalism and build trust. This helps signal that TATTVO TECH is a credible and capable IT services provider. Service-Focused Content Presentation Each service is clearly described in concise, business-friendly language. Key service categories are highlighted so that potential clients immediately understand what the company offers, reducing confusion common in multi-service firms. Prominent Call-to-Action and Easy Contact Flow A “Get in Touch / Contact Us” CTA is placed prominently (e.g., in header or home page), guiding users who want to inquire or request a quote. This reduces friction and supports lead generation by providing a straightforward path for user engagement. Responsive Design for Cross-Device Usability The layout adapts across screen sizes (desktop, tablet, mobile), ensuring readability, proper navigation, and usability regardless of device — which is crucial given clients might access the site from mobile devices. Scalable and Maintainable Structure The site’s modular structure and consistent design patterns facilitate easy updates: adding new services, updating team info, scaling as the company grows — without compromising usability or design consistency.

Outcome

Improved Clarity & User Experience — Visitors can immediately grasp what TATTVO TECH offers and how to contact the company: service categories are clear, navigation is simple, content is digestible. Enhanced Professional Impression & Credibility — The clean, modern design along with structured content helps build confidence among potential clients, positioning TATTVO TECH as a serious, reliable IT services provider. Better Conversion Potential (Leads / Client Inquiries) — With a clear contact path and prominent CTAs, the website is optimized to convert visitors into leads — improving chances of client engagement. Cross-Device Accessibility & Broad Reach — Responsive design ensures clients using mobile or desktop have equally good experience, increasing accessibility. Future-proof, Scalable Platform — The modular design and clear architecture make it easier to expand offerings, add pages, or update content as the company grows — without breaking user experience or UI consistency.

ForceInfo - UX / UI Strategy & Implementation

training / educational Website

Role: UI/UX Designer
Tools: Figma, User Research

Problem Statement

Need for Clear, Intuitive Course Discovery and Understanding — Users (prospective learners) come to the site wanting to quickly understand what courses are offered, what each course includes (learning outcomes, certification prep, hands-on practice, placement support), and whether it fits their skill / career goals. Without structured information architecture and clarity, visitors may be overwhelmed or leave without enrolling. Need for Trust & Credibility — For a training institute, users need to feel confident about legitimacy: course quality, instructor expertise, real support, value. A generic or poorly designed site may reduce trust and deter enrollment. Need for Smooth Conversion / Enrollment Flow — Visitors who are interested must easily find how to inquire, contact or sign up; friction or confusion in call-to-action (CTA), enrollment process or contact flow may cause drop-offs. Need for Responsive, Accessible & Readable Design — Prospective learners may browse from mobile devices or various screen sizes; readability, clarity, ease of navigation and responsiveness is important to ensure a good experience across devices. Need for Clear Presentation of Benefits / Value Proposition — Users need to understand not only what’s offered, but why it matters (certification, projects, career support) — ideally highlighted in a compelling, readable way.

Solution

To address the above needs and ensure the site serves its goals effectively, the following UX/UI design strategy and decisions have been implemented on ForceInfo: Clear Information Architecture & Layout The site organizes content into logical sections: e.g. “About Us / What We Offer”, “Courses” (with separate cards for each course type: Admin, Developer, Consultant), “Key Benefits”, and “Contact”. This helps prospective users quickly scan and find relevant information without confusion. Prominent Value Propositions & Benefit Highlights The homepage leads with a strong headline (e.g. “Launch Your Salesforce Career”) followed by quick statements of value: certified instructors, hands-on project-based learning, placement support, exam preparation, etc. This helps communicate benefits clearly and attract user interest. Visual & Content Hierarchy Favoring Readability Course cards, benefit sections, call-to-action links (e.g. “Learn More →”) are laid out cleanly — giving each element breathing space, making scanning easy. Good use of headings, sub-headings, images (or placeholders), and button-based navigation supports readability and ease of choice. Accessible Navigation & Conversion Flow Navigation appears straightforward (menu items like About, Courses, etc.), reducing cognitive load; CTA flows are clear (e.g. “Learn More →”), making it easier for users to proceed from browsing to inquiry/enrollment action. Responsive and Device-Friendly Approach Given the global and varied audience, design likely follows responsive practices (given that site is built on a static hosting platform), ensuring users on mobile devices have a usable experience. This is a standard good practice for services and business websites. Trust-Building Through Professional Tone & Transparency Use of professional language: course details, benefits, modules — communicates seriousness; clear presentation of what the user gets helps reduce friction and builds confidence.

Outcome

From this design and UX approach, the website for ForceInfo yields several advantages: Improved User Clarity & Engagement: Visitors immediately understand what ForceInfo offers — courses, benefits, support — reducing friction in decision making. Stronger Trust & Credibility: Clear structure, professional presentation, transparent value proposition aids in establishing legitimacy — which is crucial for educational/training services. Higher Conversion Potential: With intuitive navigation and clear CTA flows, prospective learners find it easier to take the next step (learn more / contact / enroll), improving lead generation / sign-ups. Broad Accessibility & Device Compatibility: Responsive design ensures accessibility across devices, increasing reach to users on mobile as well as desktop. Scalable & Maintainable Structure: Clean architecture supports future expansion — additional courses, updated content, new benefit sections — without compromising usability or overwhelming users.
